Grilled Chicken
Caesar Salad

The ultimate high-protein classic, reimagined for weight loss

38g Protein 420 cal 15 min prep
📊 Nutrition Per Serving
Calories420 kcal
Protein38 g
Carbs18 g
Fat20 g
Fiber4 g
Sodium580 mg
⏱️15 minPrep
🍽️1 servingYield
🌡️WarmServe
⭐EasyLevel

Ingredients

  • 200g boneless chicken breast, grilled & sliced
  • 2 cups romaine lettuce, chopped
  • 2 tbsp light Caesar dressing (store-bought or homemade)
  • 2 tbsp grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1 boiled egg, halved
  • ½ cup cherry tomatoes
  • Lemon juice, black pepper to taste

Method

  1. Season chicken breast with salt, pepper, garlic powder. Grill on medium-high heat 6–7 min per side until cooked through. Rest 3 min, then slice.
  2. Wash and chop romaine lettuce into large pieces. Place in a wide salad bowl.
  3. Add cherry tomatoes, halved boiled egg, and Parmesan cheese over the lettuce.
  4. Drizzle Caesar dressing and a squeeze of lemon juice. Toss gently to coat.
  5. Top with warm sliced grilled chicken. Season with black pepper. Serve immediately.
💡 Pro Tip Swap Parmesan for nutritional yeast to save calories while keeping the cheesy flavour. For extra crunch, add 1 tbsp toasted pumpkin seeds.

Tuna Niçoise
Power Bowl

A French-inspired lean protein masterpiece

36g Protein 390 cal 10 min prep
📊 Nutrition Per Serving
Calories390 kcal
Protein36 g
Carbs22 g
Fat14 g
Fiber5 g
Omega-31.8 g
⏱️10 minPrep
🍽️1 servingYield
🌡️Room tempServe
⭐EasyLevel

Ingredients

  • 1 can (160g) tuna in water, drained
  • 2 boiled eggs, halved
  • 1 cup green beans, blanched
  • 8–10 black olives
  • ½ cup cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 2 cups mixed greens or romaine
  • 1 tsp Dijon mustard, 1 tbsp olive oil, lemon juice, salt, pepper (dressing)

Method

  1. Boil green beans for 3–4 minutes until tender-crisp. Drain and rinse under cold water. Set aside.
  2. Whisk together Dijon mustard, olive oil, lemon juice, salt, and pepper to make the dressing.
  3. Arrange mixed greens as a base in a large bowl or plate.
  4. Artfully place tuna, halved eggs, green beans, tomatoes, and olives over the greens.
  5. Drizzle dressing generously over the salad. Season and serve fresh.
💡 Pro Tip Use tuna in water (not oil) to reduce calories. Add a small boiled potato for extra sustaining energy if you're having this post-workout.

Salmon &
Quinoa Bowl

Omega-3 rich + complete amino acid protein powerhouse

42g Protein 490 cal 20 min prep
📊 Nutrition Per Serving
Calories490 kcal
Protein42 g
Carbs32 g
Fat18 g
Fiber6 g
Omega-32.5 g
⏱️20 minPrep
🍽️1 servingYield
🌡️WarmServe
⭐MediumLevel

Ingredients

  • 150g salmon fillet, pan-seared or baked
  • ½ cup cooked quinoa
  • 2 cups arugula or baby spinach
  • ½ avocado, sliced
  • ½ cucumber, diced
  • 2 tbsp lemon-tahini dressing (1 tbsp tahini + lemon + water + garlic)
  • Salt, pepper, paprika for seasoning

Method

  1. Cook quinoa: rinse ¼ cup dry quinoa, boil in ½ cup water for 12–15 min until fluffy. Cool slightly.
  2. Season salmon with salt, pepper, paprika. Pan-sear in a non-stick pan 3–4 min per side until golden.
  3. Whisk tahini with lemon juice, a clove of minced garlic, and 2–3 tbsp water until smooth.
  4. Build the bowl: layer arugula, quinoa, cucumber, and avocado slices.
  5. Flake salmon on top. Drizzle tahini dressing. Garnish with sesame seeds if desired.
💡 Pro Tip Batch-cook quinoa for the week and store in the fridge. This makes assembly lightning-fast. Frozen salmon fillets work perfectly — thaw overnight in fridge.

Turkey Avocado
Power Salad

Lean turkey with healthy monounsaturated fats for sustained energy

35g Protein 410 cal 12 min prep
📊 Nutrition Per Serving
Calories410 kcal
Protein35 g
Carbs14 g
Fat22 g
Fiber7 g
Potassium680 mg
⏱️12 minPrep
🍽️1 servingYield
🌡️Room tempServe
⭐EasyLevel

Ingredients

  • 180g lean turkey breast, sliced or shredded
  • 1 ripe avocado, diced
  • 2 cups romaine or mixed greens
  • ½ cup corn kernels (fresh or thawed from frozen)
  • ½ cup black beans, rinsed & drained
  • ¼ red onion, thinly sliced
  • 2 tbsp lime juice, 1 tsp olive oil, cumin, salt

Method

  1. Cook turkey breast if raw: season with cumin, garlic powder, salt. Pan-cook over medium heat 5–6 min per side. Slice or shred when cool enough to handle.
  2. In a small bowl, whisk lime juice, olive oil, cumin, and a pinch of salt for the dressing.
  3. Layer romaine, black beans, corn, and red onion in a large bowl.
  4. Top with sliced turkey and diced avocado. Drizzle lime dressing over everything.
  5. Toss lightly and serve immediately. Add jalapeño for a metabolism boost.
💡 Pro Tip Buy pre-cooked or rotisserie turkey breast to cut prep time to under 5 minutes. Lime juice prevents avocado from browning if meal-prepping ahead.

Shrimp & Edamame
Asian Salad

Light, fresh, and packed with two complete protein sources

40g Protein 360 cal 15 min prep
📊 Nutrition Per Serving
Calories360 kcal
Protein40 g
Carbs20 g
Fat10 g
Fiber8 g
Sodium510 mg
⏱️15 minPrep
🍽️1 servingYield
🌡️ChilledServe
⭐EasyLevel

Ingredients

  • 150g cooked shrimp (fresh or frozen, thawed)
  • ½ cup shelled edamame (thawed from frozen)
  • 2 cups shredded purple cabbage
  • 1 cup shredded carrots
  • 2 spring onions, sliced
  • 1 tbsp sesame oil, 2 tbsp low-sodium soy sauce, 1 tsp rice vinegar, 1 tsp grated ginger (dressing)
  • 1 tsp sesame seeds, optional chilli flakes

Method

  1. If using raw shrimp, sauté in a pan with a little sesame oil, salt, and ginger for 2–3 min until pink. Set aside.
  2. Whisk sesame oil, soy sauce, rice vinegar, and grated ginger together to make the Asian dressing.
  3. Combine cabbage, carrots, edamame, and spring onions in a large mixing bowl.
  4. Pour dressing over vegetables and toss well until evenly coated.
  5. Arrange shrimp on top. Sprinkle sesame seeds and chilli flakes. Serve chilled or at room temp.
💡 Pro Tip Frozen edamame and cooked frozen shrimp make this a 5-minute meal after defrosting. Cabbage holds up well, so this salad can be meal-prepped 24 hours in advance.

Egg White
Spinach Salad

Iron-rich spinach with pure protein egg whites for fat burning

28g Protein 280 cal 10 min prep
📊 Nutrition Per Serving
Calories280 kcal
Protein28 g
Carbs12 g
Fat10 g
Iron4.2 mg
Vitamin C45 mg
⏱️10 minPrep
🍽️1 servingYield
🌡️WarmServe
⭐EasyLevel

Ingredients

  • 5 egg whites (or ¾ cup liquid egg whites)
  • 3 cups fresh baby spinach
  • ½ cup mushrooms, sliced
  • ¼ cup sun-dried tomatoes
  • 2 tbsp crumbled feta cheese
  • 1 tsp olive oil, garlic, salt, pepper
  • 1 tbsp balsamic vinegar glaze

Method

  1. Heat olive oil in a non-stick pan. Sauté mushrooms with garlic and a pinch of salt for 3–4 minutes.
  2. Pour egg whites into the pan. Scramble gently over low heat until just set. Remove from heat.
  3. Place fresh spinach in a bowl. The warmth of the eggs will lightly wilt it — that's fine.
  4. Add sun-dried tomatoes, crumbled feta, and the egg white and mushroom mixture.
  5. Drizzle balsamic glaze. Season with black pepper. Serve warm.
💡 Pro Tip Buy carton egg whites to save time separating eggs. This salad is excellent as a breakfast salad for a metabolic boost first thing in the morning.

Chickpea & Feta
Greek Salad

Vegetarian muscle food with Mediterranean flavour

26g Protein 380 cal 8 min prep
📊 Nutrition Per Serving
Calories380 kcal
Protein26 g
Carbs38 g
Fat14 g
Fiber10 g
Calcium220 mg
⏱️8 minPrep
🍽️1 servingYield
🌡️Room tempServe
⭐Very EasyLevel

Ingredients

  • 1 can (240g) chickpeas, rinsed & drained
  • 80g feta cheese, crumbled
  • 1 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
  • ½ cucumber, diced
  • ¼ red onion, thinly sliced
  • 10 Kalamata olives, pitted
  • 2 tbsp olive oil, 1 tbsp red wine vinegar, dried oregano, salt, pepper

Method

  1. Rinse chickpeas well and pat dry with a kitchen towel. This helps them absorb dressing better.
  2. Combine chickpeas, tomatoes, cucumber, red onion, and olives in a large salad bowl.
  3. Whisk olive oil, red wine vinegar, a pinch of dried oregano, salt, and pepper together.
  4. Pour dressing over salad and toss well. Let sit 3–5 minutes for flavors to develop.
  5. Crumble feta cheese on top generously. Serve with a wedge of lemon.
💡 Pro Tip For extra protein, add 2 boiled eggs or a scoop of plain Greek yogurt as a side dressing. This salad stores beautifully for up to 2 days in the fridge.

Lentil & Roasted
Veggie Salad

Plant-based protein powerhouse with warm roasted goodness

30g Protein 420 cal 25 min prep
📊 Nutrition Per Serving
Calories420 kcal
Protein30 g
Carbs48 g
Fat8 g
Fiber16 g
Iron6.6 mg
⏱️25 minPrep
🍽️2 servingsYield
🌡️Warm/coolServe
⭐MediumLevel

Ingredients

  • 1 cup dry red lentils, cooked until tender (about 15 min)
  • 1 cup bell peppers (mixed colours), diced
  • 1 zucchini, sliced
  • 1 cup cherry tomatoes
  • 2 tbsp olive oil for roasting
  • 2 cups arugula or rocket
  • Dressing: 1 tbsp tahini, lemon juice, garlic, cumin, salt

Method

  1. Preheat oven to 200°C. Toss bell peppers, zucchini, and tomatoes with olive oil, salt, and cumin. Roast 20 minutes until edges are caramelised.
  2. Meanwhile, cook lentils in boiling salted water for 15 min. Drain and cool slightly.
  3. Blend or whisk tahini dressing: combine tahini, lemon juice, minced garlic, cumin, and 3 tbsp water.
  4. Spread arugula on a large plate. Layer warm lentils and roasted veggies over the top.
  5. Drizzle tahini dressing liberally. Season and serve warm or at room temperature.
💡 Pro Tip Cook a large batch of lentils and freeze in portions. Reheat from frozen. Red lentils cook fastest of all legumes — no soaking required.

Buffalo Chicken
Ranch Salad

Spicy, satisfying, and the highest-protein recipe in this book

44g Protein 450 cal 18 min prep
📊 Nutrition Per Serving
Calories450 kcal
Protein44 g
Carbs16 g
Fat22 g
Fiber4 g
Capsaicin⚡ Fat Burn
⏱️18 minPrep
🍽️1 servingYield
🌡️WarmServe
⭐EasyLevel

Ingredients

  • 200g chicken breast, grilled & shredded
  • 3 tbsp hot sauce (Frank's or Sriracha)
  • 2 cups romaine, chopped
  • ½ cup celery, sliced
  • ¼ cup shredded carrots
  • 2 tbsp light Greek yogurt ranch (mix Greek yogurt, garlic powder, dill, onion powder)
  • 30g crumbled blue cheese or reduced-fat cheddar

Method

  1. Grill or bake chicken breast until fully cooked. Let rest 5 min, then shred with two forks.
  2. Toss shredded chicken with hot sauce in a bowl until evenly coated. Heat briefly in pan if desired.
  3. Make Greek yogurt ranch: mix 3 tbsp plain Greek yogurt with garlic powder, dried dill, onion powder, salt, and a splash of lemon juice.
  4. Build salad: romaine base, then celery, carrots, and buffalo chicken on top.
  5. Drizzle ranch dressing. Crumble cheese on top. Serve immediately while chicken is warm.
💡 Pro Tip Capsaicin in hot sauce actively boosts metabolism. Use Greek yogurt ranch instead of traditional ranch to save 120 calories while keeping the creaminess.

Cottage Cheese
Berry Power Salad

Antioxidant-rich berries with slow-digesting casein protein

32g Protein 310 cal 5 min prep
📊 Nutrition Per Serving
Calories310 kcal
Protein32 g
Carbs28 g
Fat6 g
Fiber5 g
Antioxidants⭐⭐⭐⭐
⏱️5 minPrep
🍽️1 servingYield
🌡️ChilledServe
⭐Very EasyLevel

Ingredients

  • 1 cup (225g) low-fat cottage cheese
  • ½ cup fresh strawberries, sliced
  • ½ cup fresh blueberries
  • 2 cups baby spinach or mixed greens
  • ¼ cup walnuts or almonds, roughly chopped
  • 1 tbsp honey or zero-calorie sweetener
  • 1 tsp apple cider vinegar, pinch of cinnamon

Method

  1. Wash all berries and pat dry. Slice strawberries into halves or quarters depending on size.
  2. Make the simple dressing: whisk honey (or sweetener), apple cider vinegar, and a pinch of cinnamon.
  3. Place spinach/mixed greens as the base on a plate or in a bowl.
  4. Spoon cottage cheese in the centre. Arrange berries around and over the cottage cheese.
  5. Scatter chopped walnuts over everything. Drizzle honey dressing. Serve immediately chilled.
💡 Pro Tip This is the perfect pre-bed salad. Cottage cheese contains casein protein which digests slowly overnight, supporting muscle repair and recovery while you sleep.
